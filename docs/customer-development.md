# Customer Development Plan

## Start condition

Run full customer development after Sprint 3 and Fitment F2, when a participant can complete the whole scenario:

1. upload car and rim;
2. confirm vehicle;
3. optionally add rim data;
4. receive visual fitment;
5. see preliminary technical compatibility with reasons;
6. compare original/result and leave feedback.

## Core hypotheses

- Users understand and value the separation of visual fitment and technical compatibility.
- Vehicle confirmation and optional rim details are acceptable friction.
- Compatibility information increases trust and willingness to return/pay.
- Users want compatible alternatives or specialist consultation after an incompatible/unknown verdict.

## Interview tasks

- Ask the participant to use a real or representative car/rim scenario.
- Observe without explaining the interface.
- Ask what they believe the compatibility verdict guarantees.
- Ask which fields they were willing/unwilling to enter and why.
- Ask what they would do after `unknown` or `incompatible`.
- Ask whether they would pay for visual fitment alone versus the combined outcome.

## Metrics

Track:

- create-flow completion;
- vehicle confirmation completion;
- optional rim-data completion;
- render completion and user acceptance;
- fitment verdict distribution and missing-parameter rate;
- feedback completion;
- recommendation/consultation CTA intent;
- stated and observed willingness to pay.

## Safety and communication

Use consented/test imagery. State that compatibility is preliminary and does not replace a physical installation assessment. Do not collect unnecessary personal data in interview notes.
