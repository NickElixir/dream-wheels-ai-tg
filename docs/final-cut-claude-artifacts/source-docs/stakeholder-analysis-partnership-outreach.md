# Stakeholder Analysis: Partnership Outreach

Date: 2026-05-21

This note captures an early stakeholder analysis for potential B2B partners around Dream Wheels / AI wheel visualization. It is intentionally written as a working document to revisit after MVP progress, first interviews, or early pilot results.

## Core Question

Should we contact potential B2B stakeholders before the MVP is complete?

Yes, but the message should not be framed as a finished-product sales pitch. The right framing is:

- validate interest;
- understand real workflows and objections;
- find design partners;
- collect requirements for an MVP that solves a concrete business problem;
- secure a small number of pilots or soft commitments.

The goal is not "please buy this now." The goal is "we are shaping the product around real partner needs and want to understand whether this solves an important problem for you."

## Stakeholder Segments

### 1. Tuning Studios and Customization Shops

Examples:

- https://1evel.ru/
- https://www.zimwerk.ru/msk/
- https://goshaturbotech.com/
- other tuning studios, detailing studios, styling shops, and performance-oriented auto services

Likely interests:

- helping clients visualize the final look before buying parts or approving work;
- improving conversion from "thinking about it" to "I want this";
- creating visual content for social media and sales conversations;
- offering a premium consultation experience;
- showing several styles for one customer's car quickly.

Best initial approach:

- ask for a short problem interview;
- offer to produce several free visualizations using their real cases;
- avoid asking for integration or formal partnership too early;
- position them as early design partners.

### 2. Custom Wheel Manufacturers

Example:

- https://powerwheels.ru/

Likely interests:

- showing custom wheels on a client's actual car before production;
- reducing uncertainty before an expensive custom order;
- helping customers choose design, color, fitment, and style;
- generating leads through "upload your car and see options";
- creating a stronger sales asset than generic renders or photos of similar cars.

This may be the strongest early B2B segment because custom wheels have a high imagination gap: the customer has to trust that the final result will look good before the product exists.

Best initial approach:

- propose a design-partner pilot;
- ask about their current sales process and where customers hesitate;
- offer to prepare visualizations on their wheel models or past projects;
- aim for a small pilot, not a full commercial integration.

### 3. Large Wheel and Tire Retailers

Examples:

- https://blacktyres.ru/
- https://koleso.ru/
- https://kolesa-darom.ru/
- https://svrauto.ru/
- https://shinservice.ru/
- https://mosautoshina.ru/

Likely interests:

- increasing conversion in wheel catalogs;
- reducing customer uncertainty when choosing wheels online;
- decreasing support load around fitment and style questions;
- improving product-card engagement;
- adding an AI fitting-room feature to the e-commerce journey.

Risks and barriers:

- longer sales cycles;
- need for integrations;
- requirements around catalog data, reliability, legal/privacy, metrics, and support;
- harder to reach the right decision-maker.

Best initial approach:

- do not lead with "let's collaborate";
- lead with a research hypothesis around conversion and product discovery;
- contact product, e-commerce, category management, or digital sales stakeholders;
- ask for a short expert interview;
- only discuss a pilot after there is a clear pain and a small SKU scope.

## Comparison With Avito Seller Strategy

The Avito/private-seller strategy can stay closer to:

- problem interview;
- immediate demonstration;
- transition into a sale.

That works because the sales cycle is short and the pain is direct: make the listing more attractive, sell faster, or stand out.

For companies, the strategy should be different:

- fewer messages;
- more personalization;
- more emphasis on learning;
- less pressure to buy;
- clear invitation into a pilot or design-partner role.

## Recommended Strategy

Start with the groups most likely to respond and learn quickly:

1. Custom wheel manufacturers.
2. Tuning and styling studios.
3. Smaller or niche sellers with strong social media.
4. Large retailers after there are 3-5 strong examples and clearer metrics.

The first outreach wave should target 10-15 carefully selected companies, not a broad mass email campaign.

Desired outcomes from the first 2 weeks:

- 5-10 problem interviews;
- 2-3 design-partner candidates;
- real photos and examples for testing;
- confirmation of the most valuable use case;
- better understanding of who owns the budget: owner, sales lead, marketing, e-commerce, or category manager;
- at least one quote or soft commitment such as "if this works reliably, we would be interested in testing it."

## Outreach Channel Strategy

Cold email alone will likely have low conversion. Better sequence:

1. Personalized email.
2. Follow-up after 2-3 days.
3. Telegram, WhatsApp, VK, Instagram, or site form when publicly available.
4. Short personalized visual demo when possible.
5. 15-minute call.

For each company, try to find a named person instead of writing only to a generic mailbox.

Good targets:

- founder / owner;
- commercial director;
- head of sales;
- marketing lead;
- e-commerce/product manager;
- category manager for wheels.

## What Not To Say

Avoid:

- "we are looking for cooperation" as the first message;
- "we have a revolutionary AI";
- "let's integrate";
- "we will increase your sales" without proof;
- long product explanations;
- broad generic pitch.

Prefer:

- "we are validating a hypothesis";
- "we are looking for 5-7 expert interviews";
- "we can prepare a few test visualizations for free";
- "we want to understand whether this helps with a real sales problem";
- "we are looking for 2-3 design partners."

## Two-Week Action Plan

1. Build a list of 30-50 companies: tuning studios, custom wheel manufacturers, smaller wheel sellers, larger retailers, detailing/styling studios.
2. Find a concrete person for each company where possible.
3. Use different message variants for each segment.
4. Send 10 highly personalized messages first.
5. Follow up after 2-3 days.
6. For interested companies, offer 3 free visualizations on their actual cases.
7. After each conversation, record:
   - pain;
   - current process;
   - decision-maker;
   - budget owner;
   - must-have MVP requirements;
   - whether they would test a pilot;
   - what proof they need before taking the product seriously.

## Current Best Bet

The first outreach should prioritize custom wheel manufacturers and tuning/styling studios rather than large retailers.

Large retailers are important, but they are better approached after there are several strong visual examples, clearer positioning, and early evidence that the tool can affect conversion or lead generation.
